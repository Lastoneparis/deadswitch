FAQ

Everything you need to know about DeadSwitch

How It Works
What is DeadSwitch? +

DeadSwitch is a decentralized dead man's switch for crypto. You deposit crypto into a smart contract and designate an heir. You sign a "heartbeat" monthly to prove you're alive. If you stop checking in for 90 days, Chainlink Automation triggers recovery mode and your heir can claim the funds.

How does the heartbeat work? +

Once a month (or whatever interval you set), you connect your wallet and click "I'm Still Here". This sends a transaction to the smart contract that resets the timer. It takes 5 seconds. If you forget, you'll get alerts at 30, 60, 75, and 85 days.

What happens when the owner dies? +

1. Owner stops sending heartbeats

2. After 90 days, Chainlink Automation triggers Recovery Mode

3. A 30-day cancel window starts (in case the owner is just incapacitated, not dead)

4. The heir connects their wallet, verifies with World ID, and claims the funds

5. Funds are transferred directly from the smart contract to the heir's wallet

Can I still use my crypto while it's in the vault? +

In V1, funds deposited into the vault are locked. Think of it as a safe deposit box — you put in what you want your family to inherit, not your entire portfolio. Your daily spending wallet stays separate.

In V2, we're building percentage-based inheritance — you just approve the contract as a spender and use your wallet freely. Heirs inherit a % of whatever balance exists at death. Like a real will.

Can I set multiple heirs? +

In V1: one vault, one heir. In V2: multiple heirs with custom percentages — wife.eth: 50%, son.eth: 30%, redcross.eth: 20%. Each heir claims their share independently. If an heir doesn't claim within 180 days, their share goes to a fallback beneficiary or charity.

Security
What if someone tries to claim who isn't the heir? +

Three layers of protection:

1. Pre-approved address — Only the wallet address set at vault creation can claim. The smart contract rejects all others.

2. World ID — The claimer must verify they're a real human (anti-bot). Prevents automated attacks.

3. Optional secret phrase — A shared secret only the family knows (e.g., wedding date).

An attacker would need the heir's private key + their phone (World ID) + the secret phrase. Practically impossible.

What if the owner is in a coma, not dead? +

Multiple safeguards:

• 90-day grace period — 3 months is a long time. Family, friends, or hospital staff will notice.

• 30-day cancel window — After recovery activates, the owner has 30 more days to cancel before funds move.

• Guardian (V2) — A trusted contact can pause recovery: "He's alive, just in hospital."

• Owner can resume — If the owner wakes up, they cancel recovery from any device, anytime.

What are Shamir key shards? +

Your private key is split into 5 pieces (shards) using Shamir's Secret Sharing. You need any 3 of the 5 to reconstruct the key. Each shard alone reveals zero information about the key — this is mathematically proven, not just hard to break. Shards are stored in different locations (0G storage, hardware backup, guardian) so an attacker would need to compromise 3 separate systems.

What are ZK Activity Proofs? +

Currently, heartbeats are standard on-chain transactions — everyone can see which wallet sent them. With ZK Activity Proofs (V2), the owner generates a zero-knowledge proof that says "I know the private key to this vault" without revealing which key, which wallet, or who they are. Maximum privacy — nobody can link the heartbeat to a specific person.

Technology
Which sponsors/technologies does DeadSwitch use? +

Chainlink — Automation checks heartbeats every 30 days (decentralized, unstoppable)

World ID — Verifies the heir is a real human (anti-bot)

Ledger — Hardware wallet signing for heartbeats and claims (EIP-712 Clear Signing)

0G — Encrypted key shard storage (decentralized persistence)

ENS — Human-readable: "hugo.eth → wife.eth" instead of hex addresses

Flare — TEE attestation for tamper-proof key reconstruction

Is the smart contract audited? +

The contract has 35 unit tests passing and uses OpenZeppelin's ReentrancyGuard. It's deployed on Sepolia testnet for live testing. For mainnet deployment, a formal audit by a reputable firm would be conducted first. The contract is intentionally simple — timer + transfer — to minimize attack surface.

Does DeadSwitch hold my private keys? +

No. Never. DeadSwitch is a smart contract — it holds funds, not keys. Your private key stays in your wallet (MetaMask, Ledger). The vault only stores the deposited ETH/tokens and releases them to the pre-approved heir when recovery conditions are met. No company, no server, no person ever has access to your keys.
